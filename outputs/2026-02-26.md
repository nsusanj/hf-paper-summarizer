### Bridging Efficiency and Precision for Long User Behavior Sequences

*HyTRec: A Hybrid Temporal-Aware Attention Architecture for Long Behavior Sequential Recommendation*
[Paper Link](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.18283)

Recommendation systems often struggle with long user behavior sequences. Traditional softmax attention offers precision but scales quadratically, becoming computationally prohibitive. Linear attention, while efficient, sacrifices retrieval precision by compressing too much information. This paper introduces **HyTRec**, addressing this by decoupling user behavior into short-term intent spikes and long-term stable preferences. The core idea involves a dual-branch architecture: recent interactions use standard softmax attention for high precision, while the vast historical sequence employs a hybrid attention mechanism. This hybrid branch predominantly uses a novel **Temporal-Aware Delta Network (TADN)**, a linear attention variant, strategically interleaved with a small proportion of standard softmax layers (e.g., a 7:1 ratio). TADN dynamically upweights fresh behavioral signals using an exponential temporal decay factor to capture rapid interest shifts, suppressing historical noise.

Evaluations on industrial-scale datasets like Amazon Beauty, Movies & TV, and Electronics show that HyTRec consistently outperforms strong baselines across metrics like H@500, NDCG@500, and AUC. Notably, it delivers over 8% improvement in Hit Rate for users with ultra-long sequences, all while maintaining linear inference speed. The explicit temporal modeling in TADN and the architectural balance of the hybrid attention are key to its performance. This is a practical, incremental improvement that tackles a persistent challenge in large-scale recommender systems, making long sequence modeling more feasible without sacrificing crucial accuracy.

### Generating Valid and Novel Molecular Graphs with Hierarchical Diffusion

*MolHIT: Advancing Molecular-Graph Generation with Hierarchical Discrete Diffusion Models*
[Paper Link](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.17602)

Generating novel and chemically valid molecules is a cornerstone of drug discovery and materials science. While 1D sequence-based models (like SMILES) achieve high validity, they often suffer from memorization, limiting structural exploration. Graph diffusion models, conversely, offer better exploration but frequently generate invalid structures. **MolHIT** aims to bridge this gap, allowing graph-based generation to achieve high validity without sacrificing novelty. The authors identify two key limitations in existing graph diffusion models: they treat atom categories uniformly despite chemical relationships, and naive atom encodings fail to capture critical attributes like formal charge or aromaticity.

MolHIT introduces two main components. First, a **Hierarchical Discrete Diffusion Model (HDDM)** generalizes discrete diffusion to a multi-stage process, adding mid-level states that cluster atom elements into chemical groups (e.g., {C}, {N,O,S}). This coarse-to-fine approach allows the model to learn high-level chemical identities before refining them. Second, **Decoupled Atom Encoding (DAE)** explicitly expands the atomic state space by encoding aromaticity and formal charge as primary node attributes, resolving prior reconstruction failures. The model’s forward process utilizes HDDM for atoms and a uniform transition for edges, combined with a "Project-and-Noise" sampler for enhanced diversity.

Results on the MOSES dataset demonstrate MolHIT achieving a new state-of-the-art with near-perfect validity (99.1%) and high Quality (94.2%) and Scaffold Novelty (0.39), surpassing both 1D and prior 2D models. It also performs strongly on multi-property guided generation and scaffold extension. The explicit incorporation of chemical priors through HDDM and the refined atom representation via DAE are critical for achieving this balance between validity and structural novelty. This represents a significant advancement in the fidelity and utility of graph-based generative models for chemistry.

### SkyReels-V4: A Unified Model for Cinematic Video-Audio Generation, Inpainting, and Editing

*SkyReels-V4: Multi-modal Video-Audio Generation, Inpainting and Editing model*
[Paper Link](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.21818)

Creating high-fidelity video with synchronized audio, especially with diverse conditioning, remains a complex challenge. Existing systems either handle joint audio-video generation or multimodal inputs (e.g., image/video references), but none unify all these aspects with comprehensive inpainting and editing capabilities at cinematic quality. **SkyReels-V4** is presented as a foundational model addressing this, supporting joint video-audio generation, inpainting, and editing through a single architecture. It uses a dual-stream Multimodal Diffusion Transformer (MMDiT), with parallel video and audio branches that share a powerful Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) text encoder.

A key innovation is the unified video inpainting mechanism via channel concatenation, treating tasks like image-to-video, video extension, and video editing as specific cases of inpainting. Reference images and video clips are integrated directly into the video self-attention mechanism, while offset 3D Rotary Positional Embeddings distinguish between condition and generated frames. For efficiency at 1080p resolution, 32 FPS, and 15-second duration, SkyReels-V4 employs a strategy of jointly generating low-resolution full sequences and high-resolution keyframes, followed by dedicated super-resolution and frame interpolation.

The model ranks second on the Artificial Analysis Arena leaderboard for text-to-video-with-audio. Human evaluations using SkyReels-VABench, assessed by 50 professionals, show SkyReels-V4 achieving the highest overall average score, particularly strong in prompt following and motion quality, and competitive across all other metrics against leading proprietary commercial systems. The extensive data pipeline, structured captioning, and multi-stage training are also notable. This is a very ambitious and comprehensive release, pushing the boundaries of what a single video foundation model can do.

### DreamID-Omni: Controllable Human-Centric Audio-Video Generation

*DreamID-Omni: Unified Framework for Controllable Human-Centric Audio-Video Generation*
[Paper Link](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.12160)

Human-centric audio-video generation, including tasks like reference-based generation (R2AV), video editing (RV2AV), and audio-driven animation (RA2V), have typically been approached as isolated problems. This leads to fragmented solutions and difficulties with precise control over multiple character identities and voice timbres. **DreamID-Omni** proposes a unified framework to integrate these tasks within a single model, tackling challenges like multi-person confusion and effective multi-task training. It is built on a dual-stream Diffusion Transformer with bidirectional cross-attention for video and audio synchronization.

The core of DreamID-Omni lies in its **Symmetric Conditional DiT** design, which integrates heterogeneous conditioning signals—reference images, voice timbres, source videos, and driving audio—into a shared latent space via a combination of concatenation and element-wise addition. This allows for seamless task switching. To address multi-person confusion, a **Dual-Level Disentanglement** strategy is introduced: **Synchronized RoPE (Syn-RoPE)** binds identities and timbres at the signal level by assigning distinct temporal positional segments, while **Structured Captions** establish explicit attribute-subject mappings at the semantic level using anchor tokens for each individual. A **Multi-Task Progressive Training** scheme, moving from in-pair reconstruction to cross-pair disentanglement and finally omni-task fine-tuning, is devised to harmonize conflicting learning objectives.

Extensive experiments on the new IDBench-Omni benchmark show DreamID-Omni achieving state-of-the-art performance across video, audio, and audio-visual consistency for all three tasks (R2AV, RV2AV, RA2V), even outperforming leading proprietary commercial models. The ablation studies confirm the critical roles of both Syn-RoPE and Structured Captions in preventing speaker confusion and preserving timbre. This paper offers a robust and elegant solution to the complexities of controllable human-centric multi-modal generation.

### ARLArena: Improving Stability in Agentic Reinforcement Learning

*ARLArena: A Unified Framework for Stable Agentic Reinforcement Learning*
[Paper Link](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.21534)

Agentic Reinforcement Learning (ARL) holds significant promise for solving complex, multi-step interactive tasks, particularly with the rise of LLM-based agents. However, current ARL methods are notoriously unstable, often leading to training collapse. This instability hinders scalability to larger environments and prevents systematic exploration of different algorithmic designs. This paper proposes **ARLArena**, a framework designed to provide a stable training recipe and a systematic analysis methodology for ARL.

ARLArena establishes a standardized testbed and then decomposes the policy gradient into four core design dimensions. By systematically assessing the performance and stability of each dimension, the authors distill a unified perspective on ARL instability. This analysis informs the development of **SAMPO (Stable Agentic Policy Optimization)**, a new method specifically designed to mitigate the identified dominant sources of instability in ARL. The empirical claim is that SAMPO achieves consistently stable training and strong performance across diverse agentic tasks. This work is crucial for moving ARL from a promising but fragile paradigm to a more reliable and scalable one, offering practical guidance for building robust LLM-based agent training pipelines.
